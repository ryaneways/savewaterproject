  1. What's a dual flush toilet?

    In short, it is toilet that saves a lot of water. It uses about 4 less gallons per flush than the average toilet. More specifically, a dual flush toilet has two-buttons. You push one button for a half flush which uses (.8 gallons) and another button for a full flush (1.6 gallons ).

  2. How much water does a dual flush toilet save?

    On average a dual flush toietls uses 1 gallon per flush as opposed to 5 gallons for a normal toilet. That saves 20 gallons a day, 7,000 gallons per year. If every toilet was a dual flush toilet, America would save 2 billion gallons of water each day.

  3. How can a dual flush toilet save me money?

    Toilets typically account for 30% of your home's water usage, which means a chic dual flush toilet can significantly cut down your water bill. A family of 4 can save 14,147 to 16,530 gallons of water per year and $77 to $90 each year by simply upgrading to a new toilet.

  4. Is a dual flush toilet easy to install?

    Dual flush toilets are just as easy to install as their dated counterparts, and are just as effective.

  5. Where can I get spare parts for the dual toilets?

  6. How can a dual flush toilet use less water and still get the job done?

    Dual flush toilets have improved on the typical gravity based flush by adding more force to the water as it is flushing, so it can do more with less water.
